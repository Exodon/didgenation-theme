Guidelines for DidgeNation article contributors

Please read carefully at least once (a week)

  1. Choose your strongest field in didgeridoo, and extract the most valuable points. Do not save them for later, use them for your article(s) now!
  2. If you are unsure about something, check it with someone from DidgeNation who has experience about the matter!
  3. Write in paragraphs and if possible make a title for each paragraph.
  4. Do not write very long sentences.
  5. If you write a very long article, sum up the points in the beginning under “Here’s what you need to know:” title. Example : https://www.t-nation.com/training/the-best-exercises-period – just observe the format of the first page.
  6. Some articles can be also very short, based around one specific point.
  7. Think of intriguing way to name your topics, but do not overdo the spectacle. The latter is especially true if the content doesn’t follow the greatness. Example: An article titled “Three exercises you MUST do to become a professional didgeridoo player” and in the content showing three basic exercises for circular breathing.
  8. Be careful with using of superlatives. Always question “best”, “perfect”, “epic”, “most powerful” etc.
  9. Be careful with using of commonly (over)used words like “rich in harmonics”, “concert class” etc.
  10. Be careful when you mention traditional Aboriginal ways to be respectful.
  11. Actually be respectful of everyone, without exception.
  12. Be clear if you present a) facts- name source b) your opinion c) your observation d) something else….
  13. Try to offer photos together with the article. If not possible, try to suggest what kind of photos you want to be added to your article.
  14. Try to categorize your article in terms of DidgeNation tags. This also includes difficulty level for reading. If in doubt, ask someone from DidgeNation you trust for advice!
  15. Try to see if you are doing self promotion in your article. If you do it, do it consciously and with good taste.
  16. Humour in article is more than fine, but try not to overdo it, unless you have the best day ever.
  17. Do not try to bend the didge, instead realize, there is no didge!
